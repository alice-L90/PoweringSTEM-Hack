Obesity is a Huge Concern

Obesity, also known as overweight, is when someone has a higher weight and excessive body fat. It can immensely affect a person's health as it attracts much diseases and other medical health problems. Some examples are high blood pressure, coronary heart disease, stroke, type 2 diabetes, and much more. Their BMI (Body Mass Index) measures over 30 as compared to a healthy person whose BMI is between 25 and 30.

Causes

Generally, obesity is due to the excessive calories that haven't been burned off through physical activity. Thus, it is stored as fat. Foods that can cause excessive calories are sugary foods like fast foods, soda, alcoholic drinks, sweets, etc. Other reasons include an unhealthy diet, genetic conditions, and medical reasons.

Learn more on how to keep a better diet

How To Treat Obesity

1. Discuss with your general practitioner (GP) for a plan to help you reduce weight

2. Have a healthier diet by eating fruits & vegetables, dairy foods, proteins, and wholegrains & AVOID all unhealthy foods like fast foods, sweets, and alcohol

3. Start exercising for a minimum of "150 minutes moderate-intensity activity a week" (recommended by the Chief Medical Officers). This includes any activity that can help strengthen your heart and breathing rate Ex: brisk walking, cycling, running, recreational swimming, circuit training, and dancing
