Meats

To make your favorite recipes healthier, try baking or grilling instead of frying fish or breaded chicken. You can also try using dry beans as a meat alternative. You can ask friends or search the internet and magazines for recipes with fewer calories to find new, healthier dishes to try.

Beans have many proteins, higher fibers, B-vitamins, and minerals. They are low fat and low-calorie products. A cup of 15g is double or triple the amount of rice or wheat. Also, it helps to lower the blood sugar levels and cholesterol. They are a main factor to be included in a diet plan.

Vegetables

There are many ways to prepare vegetables to add variety to your meals. You can grill or steam vegetables and add herbs like rosemary for extra flavor. You can also sauté vegetables in a non-stick pan with a small amount of cooking spray. If you need a quick side dish, you can try frozen or canned vegetables - just microwave and serve. When choosing canned vegetables, look for ones without added salt, butter, or cream sauces. You can also try a new vegetable each week to add variety to your meals.

Fruits

Fruit is a great choice for a healthy snack or part of a meal. There are many options to choose from, including fresh, frozen, or canned fruit. Some options beyond apples and bananas include mango, pineapple, and kiwi fruit. When fresh fruit is not in season, you can try a frozen, canned, or dried variety. Just be aware that dried and canned fruit may contain added sugars or syrups. Choose canned varieties that are packed in water or in their own juice.

Sugars

Good nutrition is important for maintaining overall health throughout life, but many Americans do not have a healthy diet. One issue is the high consumption of added sugars in the American diet. Added sugars are sugars and syrups that are added to foods or drinks during processing or preparation, as opposed to naturally occurring sugars found in fruits or milk. Added sugars can have many different names, such as brown sugar, corn syrup, and honey. Consuming too many added sugars can contribute excess calories to the diet without providing any essential nutrients. This can lead to health problems such as weight gain, obesity, type 2 diabetes, and heart disease.

Sugary drinks are the leading source of added sugars in the American diet so drink water!

Meal Prep

Meal prepping involves preparing a meal or recipe in advance, then dividing it into individual portions to be eaten at a later time. This can save time, money, and reduce waste in the kitchen, and is particularly convenient for those who value efficiency and control over their diet. Meal prepping is generally done for 3-7 days at a time and is best for those who don't mind eating the same things for a few days in a row. Foods that work well for meal prepping include cooked grains, pasta, beans, meat, and vegetables, whole fruit, nuts, seeds, cheese, and sauces and dips. Foods that don't usually meal prep well include softer fruits and vegetables, and crunchy items like fried foods, crackers, and chips. The length of time that meal prepped food stays "good" depends on the quality of the food when it was prepared, the way it was stored, and the individual preferences of the eater. It is important to label and date the food when storing it in the refrigerator or freezer.
